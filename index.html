July 7, 2020: Immanuel Kant

This is a random German philosopher who was a professor at the University of Königsberg. He used the term schema to mean the way a non-empirical concept is associated with the mental image of an object.
Here are some of his works:

  • Critique of Pure Reason - best known work which was followed by Critique of Judgment and Critique of Practical Reason.
    Proposed that results from geometry and simple physics might be a potential synthetic a priori truths.
    The section “The Metaphysical Deduction” explains the evolution of categories (pure concepts of understanding).
    Statements such as "the soul is simple" and "the soul is a substance" are "paralogisms" of its title subject.
    A priori judgments debunk proof of God’s existence.
  • Perpetual Peace - a world without war based on constitutional republics
  • Groundwork of the Metaphysics of Morals - outlined the categorical imperative
    (one should only act if that action should become universal law)
  • Answering the Question: What is Enlightenment? - he used the phrase sapere aude (dare to know)
    saying that one should think autonomously
  • Inaugural Essay - difference between intellectual thought and sensible receptivity

July 7, 2020: Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel

German philosopher, founder of absolute idealism. Influenced Karl Marx

  • Hegel Dialectic - describes the synthesis resolving the conflict between the inevitable antithesis and the pre-existing thesis.
    Also describes development in 3 stages: in itself, out of itself, and in and for itself (thesis, antithesis, and synthesis)
  • The Phenomenology of Spirit - describes a collective consciousness called the geist and the development of self-consciousness
    out of two forces in the master-slave dialectic (common name for one famous passage)
  • The Philosophy of History - argued that “the State is actually an existing realized moral life"
  • The Positivity of the Christian Religion
  • Elements of the Philosophy of Right - discussed the concept of Sittlichkeit (ethical life) and
    criticized a work by Karl Ludwig von Hallerby
  • Encyclopedia of the Philosophical Sciences which includes some of his earlier The Science of Logic

July 8, 2020: Friedrich Nietzsche

German philosopher, the youngest to hold the Chair of Classical Philology. At 44, he suffered a loss of his mental faculties.
Attributed his wisdom to the sensitivity of the weight of others. Eternal reoccurrence is his idea that with infinite time and a finite number of events, they will reoccur indefinitely.

  • Thus Spoke Zarathustra - discusses the ubermensch (ideal future man)
  • Beyond Good and Evil - suggested the “will to power” explains all human behavior
  • Ecce Homo
  • The Gay Science - claimed that “God is Dead” and called amor fati the “formula for greatness in a human being”
  • Daybreak - altruism arises from low self-esteem
  • Untimely Meditations - collection of short works
  • The Birth of Tragedy - compared writings of Euripides between Dionysian Apollonian works
    • Human, All too Human - collection of aphorisms dedicated to Voltaire where he argued that great art is the result of hard work,
    not genius or divine inspiration.
  • On the Genealogy of Morals - identified the master and slave forms of morals

July 10, 2020: René Descartes

French philosopher and mathmatician who believed that the pineal gland was the principal seat of the soul and the place
where all of our thoughts are formed.

  • Discourse on Method - cogito, ergo sum
  • Regulae (Rules for the Direction of the Mind) - written during his 20 years in the Netherlands
  • Le Monde - Based on the studies of Copernicus which were hidden after Galileo's persecution
  • Passions of the Soul - written in French while tutoring the Swedish Queen Christina

July 11, 2020: Soren Aabye Kierkegaard

A Danish philosopher, fervent Christian, and influential to existentialism.
Wrote his works from several viewpoints to show various styles of thinking.
Dread plays an important role in his thought and the highest stage of human existence is reached through overcoming it through a paradoxical thought process.
He felt that modern society reduced the meaning of the individual.

  • The Sickness unto Death - written under the pseud “Anti-Climacus” and discussed the threshold for when a human is in despair
    when not aligned with God. The title is taken from the Gospel of John
  • Either/Or -
  • Fear and Trembling - discusses the “teleological suspension of the ethical” on whether Abraham was justified in sacrificing Isaac.
    Written under the pseudonym John the Silent and published the same year as Either/Or. Ends with a discussion of Heraclitus.
    3 chapters titled Problema explain the actions of the knight of faith as contrasted to the knight of infinite resignation.
  • The Book on Adler - criticized the title figure for claiming to have revelations from God

July 8, 2020: Joan Miró

Spanish Surrealist painter, sculptor, and ceramicist. He met Picasso, the surrealists, and some Dadaist artists in Paris.
During the Spanish Civil War, his art went into a "wild period." A museum is dedicated to him in his hometown of Barcelona.
He also made over 250 illustrated books and in later years, explored gas sculpture.

  • The Farm
  • Constellations (series)
  • Lunar Bird
  • The Hunter (Catalan Landscape)
  • The Harlequin's Carnival
  • Wall of the Moon and Wall of the Sun - ceramic murals at the UNESCO building in Paris

July 9, 2020: El Greco

Born on Crete, his real name was Domenikos Theotókopoulos. He was influenced by Titian and Tintoretto and settled in Toledo, Spain.

  • El Espolio (The Disrobing of Christ) - shows Christ being strupped of his clothing before crucifixion.
    He is peaceful compared to the ugly faces and violence around him. The 3 Marys are in the left foreground.
  • The Martyrdom of St. Maurice - Maurice is in a blue breastplate and red tunic surrounded by legion officers.
    They decide whether to sacrifice to the pagan gods or accept martyrdom.
  • The Burial of the Count of Orgaz - divided into 2 sections: the heavens above and the terrestrial below.
    Saints Stephen and Augustine have arrived to bury the Count after his murder. Commissioned by Andrés Nuñes.
  • View of Toledo - vibrant and unusual landscape with the Castle of San Servando in the background.
  • Opening of the Fifth Seal - previously called Profane Love, it was unfinished and damaged.
    The subject is from Revelation with the souls of martyrs crying out to God for justice. St. John strangely points
    at nowhere, with naked souls behind him.
